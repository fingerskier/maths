# Curriculum Design Notes

## Guiding Principles

1. **Progressive leveling** — Each level builds on the previous. You must complete Level N before Level N+1.
2. **Implement to learn** — Every concept is tied to a concrete coding problem. No passive reading.
3. **Test-driven** — pytest validates your implementations against known correct answers.
4. **From scratch** — Implement algorithms yourself before using library functions. Understand the math, don't just call scipy.

## Topic Coverage

### Pure Mathematics Foundation (Levels 0-1)
- Logic, proof writing, number theory, sets, basic algebra
- Discrete math, combinatorics, graph theory
- Boolean algebra, lattices, posets

### Analysis and Computation (Levels 2-3)
- Multivariable calculus, ODEs, PDEs
- Linear, nonlinear, and gradient-based optimization

### Probability and Numerical Methods (Levels 4-5)
- Probability theory, stochastic processes, Markov chains
- Statistical inference (frequentist and Bayesian)
- Root finding, interpolation, numerical integration
- Sparse linear algebra, FFT, Monte Carlo

### Machine Learning and Advanced Methods (Levels 6-7)
- Classification, clustering, dimensionality reduction
- Neural networks and automatic differentiation
- Gaussian processes, variational inference
- Uncertainty quantification, inverse problems
- Physics-informed neural networks, Bayesian optimization

### Applied Domains (Level 8)
- Computational fluid dynamics
- Computational biology (population dynamics, sequence alignment)
- Energy systems (power flow, renewable optimization)

## Future Directions
- LEAN proof tracks for Levels 0-1 (formal verification)
- Julia implementations for numerical performance comparison
- Interactive notebooks for visualization-heavy problems
